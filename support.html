Office of Diversity, Inclusion, and Multicultural Education and LGBTQ+ Resources (ODIME & LGBTQ+)

ODIME works with many Mason entities and students to foster and sustain an inclusive and culturally aware campus. Various student communities - such as Asian-Pacific American Coalition (APAC), Hispanic Latino Leadership Association (HLLA), Queer Trans People of Color (QTPOC), and the Black Leadership Roundtable (BLR) of Black Mason - often times find support and advising in this office.

A great thing about ODIME is that it operates as a hangout space for many LGBTQ+ people and POC who need somewhere to decompress and feel understood. Also, many of the faculty and students here are usually equipped to understand the challenges and joys that come with our identities, so you can definetly find a listening, supportive ear here.

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

Mental health is becoming more and more important in the black community. CAPS serves as an entry point for students to acquire off-campus services with their insurance and/or FREE on-campus psychological services by quality-trained counselors and clinicians. Everything is confidential, so only you and your counselor will know what you discuss

Some great services include group therapy, workshops, psychiatric evaluations, and online self-help - fight the stigma against mental health and take advantage of these services. Also, try out the MindSpa, a relaxation room with essential oils, music, and a massage chair to destress after a long week.

Student Support and Advocacy Center (SSAC)

SSAC helps students develop and maintain healthy lifestyles by addressing some of the most challenging issues they may face during their collegiate years:

  • Healthy relationships
  • Sexual assault
  • Drug and alcohol use
  • Nutrition and body image
  • Stress management
  • Sexual health

The office provides educational programming, one-on-one support and counseling, group support, and advocacy services - students are most successful when healthy and happy and we support them in finding that balance for themselves. The office connects students with appropriate Mason and off-campus resources and implements action plans for students in need of intervention, including crisis intervention. Student Health Services (SHS)

Our physicians and nurse practitioners provide easily accessible health care in a comforting and confidential environment. We provide care for a range of needs, including diagnosing and treating injuries and illness, providing immunizations, managing chronic health concerns, and conducting physical exams. A nurse advice line is available when the clinic is closed, via 703-993-2831. There is no charge to see a health care provider, but there are fees for lab tests, medications, immunizations, and supplies.

Collegiate Black Men (CBM)

The mission of Black Mason is to develop an open and accepting community of black men, who are academically accomplished, socially responsible, and career-ready.

There are many pressures that come with being a black man in America that often hold us back from being the figures of our communities that we are meant to be. CBM is dedicated to unpacking much of the generational and societal stressors that affect black men while also equipping them with the tools and sense of community to make them better leaders, working-men, fathers, husbands, boyfriends, and so on.
